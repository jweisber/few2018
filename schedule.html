Formal Epistemology Workshop 2018

Schedule

In addition to invited talks by Lara Buchak and Mike Titelbaum, the following submitted papers will be given:

  • "Can Accuracy Motivate Modesty?" , by Jennifer Carr
  • "Ungrounded Payoffs. A Tale of Unconditional Love and Unrepentant Hate" , by Eleonora Cresto
  • "Reducing Contrastive Knowledge" , by Michael Cohen
  • "Concept Utility" , by Paul Egre & Cathal O'Madagain
  • "Fragmentation, Mathematical Ignorance, and the Metalinguistic Reply" , by Adam Elga & Agustin Rayo
  • "'Ramseyfying' Probabilistic Comparativism" , by Edward Elliott
  • "Hindsight Bias is not a Bias" , by Brian Hedden
  • "An Objection of Varying Importance to Epistemic Utility Theory" , by Ben Levinstein
  • "Modes of Convergence to the Truth: Steps toward a Better Epistemology of Induction" , by Hanti Lin
  • "Knowability as Learning" , by Ivan Verano
Exact times and dates are still TBD.
